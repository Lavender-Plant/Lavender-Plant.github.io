Lavender

The Fragrant Purple Herb

About Lavender

Lavender is a flowering plant in the mint family known for its calming scent and beautiful purple flowers. It is widely grown for ornamental, culinary, and medicinal purposes.

Main Types of Lavender

1. English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

English lavender is the most common and popular type. It has a sweet, floral scent and is often used for essential oils, perfumes, and cooking. It grows well in cooler climates and blooms in late spring to early summer.

2. French Lavender (Lavandula dentata)

French lavender has toothed leaves and a lighter fragrance. It blooms for a long season and is often grown as a decorative plant. It prefers warmer climates.

3. Spanish Lavender (Lavandula stoechas)

Spanish lavender is known for its unique flower shape, which looks like it has small “rabbit ear” petals on top. It has a stronger scent and thrives in hot, dry climates.

4. Lavandin (Lavandula x intermedia)

Lavandin is a hybrid of English and spike lavender. It produces larger plants and more oil, making it popular for commercial production. Its scent is slightly stronger and more camphor-like.

Uses of Lavender

  • Aromatherapy and relaxation
  • Essential oils and perfumes
  • Cooking and baking
  • Natural insect repellent
  • Decorative garden plant
